# DEC-UX-002 — Homepage Experimental Editorial Hybrid

ID: `DEC-UX-002`
Title: Homepage Experimental Editorial Hybrid
Status: **Approved Decision**
Decision date: 13 July 2026; reconfirmed 23 July 2026
Decision source: `docs/decisions/evidence/HOMEPAGE_PROTOTYPE_DECISION.md`; explicit user decision recorded during documentation consolidation, 23 July 2026.

## Context

The Niuva Brand-to-Website Audit found that the public website was credible but visually repetitive: similar heroes, repeated labels, card-heavy grouping, recurring blue proof panels, and decorative motifs made distinct pages feel templated.

Two isolated Homepage prototypes were compared:

- Editorial Product Studio emphasized clarity, open project presentation, calm pacing, and typographic restraint.
- Experimental Engineering Studio emphasized technical personality, differentiated capability chapters, and a connected transformation path.

Neither prototype was selected unchanged. The approved outcome combines the strengths of both while preserving Niuva's official identity, factual content, accessibility, and B2B credibility.

## Decision

The Homepage visual direction is **Experimental Editorial Hybrid**.

It includes:

- technical personality and transformation logic from Experimental Engineering Studio;
- editorial clarity, readable hierarchy, open project presentation, and restraint from Editorial Product Studio;
- Poppins for display, headings, navigation-style UI, and buttons;
- Inter for body copy, labels, captions, forms, and public metadata;
- authentic project evidence presented as context, challenge, method or solution, output, and capability proven;
- differentiated capability chapters rather than identical service cards;
- a semantic U-curve representing `Need → Research → Experiment → Prototype → Output`;
- exactly two dominant U-curve placements in the initial Homepage: compact hero path and complete process path;
- public motion intensity no greater than 5/10;
- a complete static equivalent for reduced-motion users;
- an explicit rejection of card-heavy, generic SaaS, generic vendor, and AI-generated Homepage composition.

## Rationale

- Niuva already communicates professionalism; the larger gap is distinctiveness and visible product-development personality.
- Project evidence and transformation logic provide stronger technical credibility than decorative technology effects.
- Poppins maintains continuity with the official brandmark, while Inter improves body and metadata clarity.
- A semantic U-curve turns an existing brand element into an understandable process model.
- Open editorial project layouts make evidence more credible than enclosing every project in a repeated card.
- Restrained motion preserves accessibility, performance, and content access.

## Consequences

- Homepage composition must be purpose-built around narrative hierarchy rather than assembled from repeated generic cards.
- Research & Development and Design & Prototyping remain visually dominant.
- Consultant & Workshop and Apparel & Merchandise remain supporting.
- The official `ni` brandmark must be used; a technical “N” is not an acceptable company logo.
- Monospace cannot be used as a decorative public technical voice.
- The Homepage implementation plan must also be reconciled with `DEC-UX-001` before implementation.
- Missing authentic process photography remains a declared dependency rather than a reason to fabricate imagery.

## Constraints

- Do not use gradients, neon, glassmorphism, particles, decorative telemetry, fake dashboards, or generic futuristic effects.
- Do not repeat U-curves as wallpaper, card ornaments, navigation decoration, footer decoration, or text backgrounds.
- Do not use Manrope + Space Mono, Space Mono, or JetBrains Mono as the production Homepage typography system.
- Do not make motion necessary to understand content or reach an action.
- Do not invent project media, process artifacts, clients, metrics, awards, certifications, testimonials, or outcomes.
- Do not upscale low-resolution project imagery beyond an appropriate evidence role.
- Do not copy the public Homepage composition into Admin Studio.
- Do not treat this decision as authorization to modify source code or another public route.

## Deferred Details

This decision does not determine:

- detailed Retail/B2B navigation treatment;
- exact animation timing and implementation library;
- permanent shared-component API changes;
- broader typography rollout beyond the approved Homepage scope;
- visual rollout to About, Capabilities, Projects, or Contact;
- Brand Guidelines v1.1 publication;
- service taxonomy rename or Office & Signage placement;
- process-photography acquisition schedule;
- production implementation authorization.

## Superseded Statements

Within the Homepage scope, this decision supersedes:

- selecting Concept A or Concept B unchanged;
- treating Concept B as the final production composition without Editorial Product Studio controls;
- the Manrope + Space Mono production experiment;
- Poppins-only public body typography for the Homepage;
- JetBrains Mono as public Homepage technical styling;
- decorative or repeated U-curve usage;
- identical card treatment for primary capabilities;
- any implementation guidance that uses a technical “N” as the company identity.

The Brand Guidelines, prototype review, and implementation plan remain useful references within their recorded scopes; they are not superseded in full.
