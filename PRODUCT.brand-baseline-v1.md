# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Primary users are companies, institutions, and industry teams looking for a partner in R&D, design engineering, prototyping, EV/product development, simulators, or custom technical products. They need fast evidence that NIUVA is credible, experienced, and easy to contact.

Secondary users are startups, universities, research institutions, innovation communities, makers, and training organizations seeking idea validation, early prototypes, workshops, consulting, or research collaboration.

Tertiary users are brands, communities, event organizers, and marketing teams looking for apparel, merchandise, brand identity products, or event-related creative production.

The current implementation also includes authenticated client and admin users for the 3D printing order platform. Those operational surfaces should serve task completion first: order creation, status tracking, estimation, payment-proof handling, fulfillment, materials, portfolio, users, contacts, internships, and settings.

## Product Purpose
NIUVA's website is the official digital presence for PT Niuva Inovasi Utama, positioning the company as a strategic partner for turning ideas into real products through research, design, engineering, prototyping, consulting, workshops, and integrated creative solutions.

The primary business outcome is B2B lead generation: visitors should understand NIUVA's capabilities, trust its portfolio, and contact the team through WhatsApp, email, consultation forms, or project discussion CTAs. The current product scope extends that lead path into a 3D printing service workflow with client order tracking and admin operations.

Success means the site can be used in pitching, proposals, networking, and sales conversations as a credible official reference, while the authenticated platform helps clients and admins complete service workflows clearly and reliably.

## Brand Personality
NIUVA should feel professional, innovative, clean, technical, trustworthy, collaborative, and precise.

The voice is confident and clear without sounding overly promotional. Technical terms are welcome when they clarify expertise, but the copy should remain understandable to business and institutional decision makers. The brand should communicate strategic partnership, not commodity production.

## Anti-references
Do not make NIUVA look like a generic vendor, a static PDF company profile converted to a web page, or a cheap "best and cheapest service" marketplace pitch.

Avoid presenting all services with equal weight. R&D and Design & Prototyping are the face of the business; Consultant & Workshop and Apparel & Merchandise are supporting capabilities in the wider innovation ecosystem.

Avoid overpromising, overpromotional copy, generic tech-startup claims, weak portfolio lists without business context, e-commerce-first framing, blog-first framing, and visual choices that bury the path to discuss a project.

For operational dashboards, avoid decorative brand theatrics that slow down order management, payment verification, or admin decision-making.

## Design Principles
Show capability through evidence. Portfolio and project content should behave like mini case studies: challenge, solution, output, and the capability proven.

Lead with R&D and prototyping. Visual hierarchy, navigation, and CTAs should make the core business immediately legible before introducing supporting services.

Make contact obvious. The site should repeatedly and clearly route visitors toward discussing a project through WhatsApp, email, forms, and focused CTAs.

Balance technical confidence with business clarity. NIUVA should feel precise and engineering-capable, but not so technical that decision makers struggle to understand value.

Separate brand impact from operational efficiency. Public pages can carry stronger brand expression; client and admin surfaces should prioritize clarity, status, data density, and task completion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Use Indonesian as the primary language while allowing relevant English technical terms such as Research & Development, Design & Prototyping, Workshop, Engineering, Prototype, Capabilities, and Project. Where bilingual ID/EN UI exists, keep both languages complete enough for core navigation and workflows.

Text must be easy to read, contrast must be sufficient, and buttons or CTAs must be visually clear. The site should work comfortably on desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences. Forms, uploads, dashboard states, and status tracking should remain understandable without relying only on color, animation, or icons.
